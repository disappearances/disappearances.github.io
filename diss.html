Disappearance reports

This section contains reports by state appointed mechanisms relating to the issue of disappearances in Sri Lanka. The mechanisms include Presidential Commissions of Inquiry and other mechanisms .

Reports of Commissions of Inquiry

The material on this page relates to Presidential Commissions of Inquiry that were appointed under the Commission of Inquiry Act No 17 of 1948.

CBK Commissions â€“ Zonal and All Island Commissions

In November 1994, President Kumaratunga divided the country into three areas and appointed three concurrent Presidential Commissions of Inquiry. The Commissions are commonly known as the Zonal Commissions. By warrant dated 30 April 1998, the subsequent All Island Commission looked into complaints of disappearances that the Zonal Commissions were unable to look into.

Northern Commission

The Northern Commission looked into complaints of disappearances from the Northern and Eastern Provinces. The Chair was Krishnapillai Palakidner. The final report is dated September 1997

Southern Commission

The Southern Commission looked into complaints of disappearances in the Western, Southern, and Sabaragamuwa Provinces. The Chair was Manouri Kokila Muttetuwegama. The final report is dated September 1997.

Central Commission

The Central Commission looked into complaints of disappearances from the Central, North Western, North Central, and Uva Provinces. The Chair was Thirunavakkarasu Suntheralingam. The final report is dated July 1997.

All Island Commission

In April 1998, President Kumaratunga appointed a further (fourth) Presidential Commission of Inquiry to look into complaints of disappearances that the three Zonal Commissions were unable to look into. The Chair was Manouri Kokila Muttetuwegama. The final report is dated March 2001.

Tilakaratne Commission

In September 2006, President Rajapakshe appointed a one-man Presidential Commission of Inquiry. The Chair was Mahanama Tilakaratne. The final report is dated May 2007.

Paranagama Commission

In August 2013, President Rajapakshe appointed a Presidential Commission of Inquiry. The first mandate related to disappearances. The mandate was expanded in 2014. The second mandate related to violations of international law during the armed comflict. The Chair was Maxwell Paranagama. The interim report of the first mandate is dated August 2015. The final report of the second mandate is dated August 2015.
